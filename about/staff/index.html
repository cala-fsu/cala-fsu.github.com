Our Staff

Our team of specialists—most with advanced degrees—provides diverse expertise in research, education, instructional design, assessment and evaluation, psychometrics, software engineering, writing, editing, graphic design, and web and multimedia development.

Staff Biographies

  • Laura Adams, program coordinator. Laura is responsible for coordinating activities related to CALA’s assessment projects and for processing all payroll and personnel activities. A graduate of Florida State University (FSU), she has been with CALA since March 2012. Away from the office, Laura enjoys live music and reading.
  • Lillian Caldwell, office assistant. Lilly has worked with CALA for about a year while studying at FSU. She assists with office activities and other daily tasks such as filing, scanning, and delivering documents to clients around the Tallahassee area. In her free time, Lilly can be found playing Ultimate Frisbee for the FSU women’s club team.
  • Sean G. Coleman, lead software designer. Sean leads his technically savvy team through CALA’s various software projects, from Ruby on Rails websites to data analysis programs. An advocate of web standards, usability, and accessibility in web design, Sean makes sure our work reaches the largest audience. When he’s not wriggling with code, Sean can be found on the local public tennis courts grinding down his opponents.
  • Michael Davidson, software engineer. Michael develops software solutions for the technological needs of CALA. He has been developing software for CALA for nearly 10 years and has been involved in the software development industry for more than 15 years. Because he creates game applications for instruction and assessment, Michael is one of the few on staff who gets paid to play games.
  • Colin Dwyer, senior graphic designer. Colin has worked in graphic design and illustration for more than 20 years, half of that time with CALA. He has designed various publications, displays, websites, and multimedia products. His ability as an illustrator sets him apart from other designers and is a skill commonly used by clients. Using his audio recording experience, he recorded and mixed the audio in CALA’s e-learning courses. Colin has an array of interests, but his candy-making phase was a notable favorite.
  • Alice Fisher, senior production editor. Alice puts the finishing touch on all deliverables, ensuring the quality of content and design. She has edited educational and technical materials at CALA for more than 30 years. Trained in test measurement editing, she has an extensive knowledge of editorial style and production. If a comma goes missing, Alice will find it. When not at CALA, Alice is petting the neighborhood cat and embracing change.
  • Karen Hawkins, senior writer and development editor. Karen guides an array of print and online materials through the creative process, from writing e-learning scripts to editing research reports. As a wordsmith, she brings a love of language and 20 years of publishing experience to CALA. When she's not mending split infinitives, Karen can be found reading British literature and quoting Shakespeare to anyone who will listen.
  • Adam LaMee, graduate research assistant. Adam is pursuing graduate study in experimental high energy physics at FSU. As part of the CALA team, he researches science competencies that go unassessed in statewide high-stakes tests, drafting alternative performance assessments and acting as a liaison with the project’s participating middle school teachers. When he isn’t smashing protons in Switzerland, Adam enjoys learning to play new musical instruments and tinkering on his 1957 Chevy Belair.
  • Albert Oosterhof, professor emeritus. Al was a faculty member for 35 years in the Educational Psychology and Learning Systems Department at FSU. He is the author of several prominent books on assessment and has developed a cognitive complexity model. If he’s not working on cognition, Al is building a kayak from scratch or going on safari in Africa.
  • Kathy Pennington, project manager. Kathy joined CALA in 2000 as a student assistant and has ascended the ranks to her current position. As a project manager, she oversees project planning, staffing, budgeting, and quality assurance. In her spare time, Kathy volunteers at the local animal shelter.
  • Faranak Rohani, director. Fara’s dream is to bring CALA’s mission to reality while providing a nurturing and team-oriented working environment for the staff. Her career over the past quarter of a century has been devoted to research dealing with the nature of knowledge and effective instruction and assessment. When not doing research, Fara is known for planning everyone’s life and for her desire to improve her tennis game so she can appear on a court with Sean.
  • Aaron Rouby, graduate research assistant. Aaron develops performance assessments, scoring rubrics, and training materials related to CALA’s science assessment project. He has a master’s degree in social psychology, background in research methods and statistics, and is currently pursuing a degree in visual disabilities. In his free time, Aaron enjoys playing guitar and basketball.
  • Danielle Sherdan, science expert. Danielle is part of the research team that is investigating ways to improve how students are assessed in middle school science. She has worked as a project manager and science specialist at the Florida Center for Research in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics at FSU and has received awards for teaching and research. When not working, Danielle is chasing the bats and birds that are chasing the beetles from mango tree to mango tree in Brasília, Brazil.
  • Sweta Shrestha, application designer and developer. Sweta’s main responsibility is to write and run programs for scoring the Florida Comprehensive Assessment Test. She majored in computer science and enjoys database and parallel programming. In her spare time, Sweta can be found cheering on the Chelsea FC.
  • Peggy Stillwell, training specialist. Peggy develops and delivers training to content specialists and consults with other CALA staff on test development issues. She has been involved in the development of statewide high-stakes reading tests for departments of education in several states, including Florida, Georgia, Ohio, Indiana, West Virginia, Maryland, New York, and Colorado. Reading truly is “in her blood.”

In Memoriam

FJ King (February 4, 1927–April 21, 2011), professor emeritus. FJ was a faculty member for 50 years in the Educational Psychology and Learning Systems Department at FSU. For nearly half that time, he served as a member of our research team. He was a nationally known expert in assessment, with numerous published articles in his field. His final research was related to computer-written versus handwritten essays. His passion was to ride his antique tractor while puffing on his pipe.
