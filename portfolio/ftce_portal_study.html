A Single Source

FTCE Portal Development

At CALA, we use advanced technologies to provide broad access to information. For individuals interested in teaching in Florida, we developed a single, integrated online site that provides wide-ranging resources related to teacher certification.

The Challenge

The Florida Department of Education (FLDOE) commissioned CALA to develop a Web portal for the Florida Teacher Certification Examinations (FTCE). The portal had to be appropriate for a variety of audiences, including teacher candidates, teachers expanding certification, and career changers, and had to meet rigorous usability and accessibility standards. FLDOE required that the design be consistent with its new Web site that was being implemented at the time. Our desire was to engage users and provide critical information in an easily navigable manner. To accomplish this task, CALA staff had to organize diverse and comprehensive sets of information in a logical, meaningful way for the intended audience.

The Solution

In gathering current and relevant resources for the portal, we relied on the expertise of educators in colleges, universities, and Educator Preparation Institutes (EPI) across the state. We contacted deans, associate deans, department heads, and EPI directors, requesting their help in obtaining a list of resources from faculty members and subject-matter experts that were used in their teacher preparatory programs. To facilitate the response process, we created a password-protected, database-driven Web site where these resources could be uploaded. We verified all links and edited descriptions for quality and consistency.

We wrote descriptions highlighting the requirements for each of the 44 FTCE tests and offered downloadable competencies and skills for each examination. Additional information included certification requirements, types and lists of educator preparation programs, testing requirements, test information guides, and registration for certification examinations.

We built the portal using Web standards-compliant XHTML and CSS. The server-side code was written in C# on the ASP.NET platform, and an SQL Server database was used for storage. We tested the portal’s usability and accessibility to ensure that it reaches the largest possible audience.

During the course of the project, we worked closely with FLDOE assessment, Web development, and Blind Services groups to guarantee all elements of the portal met FLDOE specifications. We conducted focus groups with the target audiences and incorporated their feedback into the final product.

The Results

The portal, which resides on the FLDOE Web site successfully integrates diverse information into a single comprehensive source. It gives individuals seeking teacher certification the tools they need to prepare for tests in their content area. From a design perspective, it integrates nicely into the FLDOE Web site. The portal will be used as a model for accessible Web development for future FLDOE projects.
